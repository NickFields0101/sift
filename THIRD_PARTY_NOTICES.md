# Third-party notices

## Johnson IPIP-NEO-120

SIFT optionally includes the **Johnson (2014) IPIP-NEO-120**, a 120-item representation of the Five-Factor Model of personality. It reports the five broad Big Five/OCEAN domains and 30 facets. Typical completion time in SIFT is approximately 15–20 minutes.

The International Personality Item Pool (IPIP) items and scales are in the public domain. The IPIP states that its items may be used, copied, edited, translated, or otherwise adapted without permission or a royalty. SIFT nevertheless identifies the instrument and source so results remain interpretable and auditable.

Primary references:

- Johnson, J. A. (2014). Measuring thirty facets of the Five Factor Model with a 120-item public domain inventory: Development of the IPIP-NEO-120. *Journal of Research in Personality, 51*, 78–89. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jrp.2014.05.003
- [IPIP public-domain permission statement](https://ipip.ori.org/newPermission.htm)
- [Official IPIP-NEO item and scoring key](https://ipip.ori.org/30FacetNEO-PI-RItems.htm)
- [IPIP scoring instructions](https://ipip.ori.org/newScoringInstructions.htm)

SIFT scores each response according to the published positive/reverse-keyed item direction, combines four items per facet, and combines six facets per broad domain. The interface converts the possible raw range to a 0–100 response-scale position for readability. These values are **not normative percentiles** and should not be interpreted as a comparison with the general population.

The assessment is offered for self-reflection and optional idea personalization. It is not the proprietary NEO Personality Inventory-3 (NEO-PI-3), is not a clinical or diagnostic instrument, and must not be used for hiring or other consequential decisions.
